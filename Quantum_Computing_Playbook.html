Quantum Technology Types
💡

Why Technology Type Matters

Quantum computers work differently depending on their qubit technology. Each approach has tradeoffs between error rates, scalability, operating temperature, and commercial readiness. You are essentially betting on which technology wins the race to fault-tolerant quantum computing.

IONQ

⚛️ Trapped-Ion

Uses laser-cooled ytterbium atoms as qubits. Best error rates in the industry — key metric for practical use. Scales via networking multiple processors. Industry-leading fidelity. Requires cryogenic-level isolation but not dilution refrigerators.

RGTI · IBM · GOOGL

🌀 Superconducting

Uses superconducting circuits cooled to near absolute zero (-273°C). Most widely researched approach. IBM Eagle/Heron and Google Willow are superconducting. Fastest gate speeds. Main challenge: very short coherence times require quantum error correction.

QBTS

🔄 Quantum Annealing

Specialized for optimization problems — finds the lowest energy state of a system. Not a universal quantum computer but commercially deployed TODAY for logistics, finance, and drug discovery. Only approach with proven commercial clients.

QUBT

📸 Photonic / Nanophotonic

Uses photons (light particles) as qubits. Key advantage: operates at room temperature — no cryogenic cooling required. QUBT's Fab 1 foundry manufactures photonic chips using standard semiconductor supply chains. Earliest commercial stage.
